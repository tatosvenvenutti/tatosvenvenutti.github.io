Knight AI

Originally, our game featured only zombies, but I decided to tackle AI. Creating the knights, from their attacks to behavior trees, was immensely satisfying. I wanted them to have combos, charge attacks, and counters, and I'm thrilled I made it happen with my skills.

  • Designed and developed actors and behavior trees
  • Implemented blocking, combos, charge attacks, and patrolling features in C++
  • Created customizable weapons, enabling each enemy to have different weapons

Boss AI

This was the first enemy I developed, and a boss no less. I wanted him to be more defensive and almost territorial, where he shoots projectiles to make you push him and then use large sweeping attacks to make you keep your distance. This created an almost back-and-forth rhythm of retreating and advancing.

  • Developed a defensive, territorial boss enemy
  • Designed projectile attacks to push players and large sweeping attacks to maintain distance
  • Created a rhythmic gameplay of retreating and advancing for the boss encounter

Non-Hostile AI

To bring more life to the open world, I decided at the last minute to add basic animal AI for deer, pigs, wolves, and foxes. Although it wasn't part of the original plan, I wanted to give it a try.

  • Implemented actors and behavior trees
  • Developed and integrated AI for deer, pigs, wolves, and foxes to enhance world immersion
  • Programmed dynamic behaviors to increase environmental realism

References and Inspirations

With the help of my fantastic team, our goal was to create a large world like Elden Ring, with the combo strings of Monster Hunter and the fluidity of Vindictus.

Combat Design

Wanted to emulate Vindictus in its weight based and slashing motions.

Player Super

What fantasy world would be complete without super abilities? While I helped design the players' abilities, I coded the hammer ability for the first playable character. It's a powerful move where he strikes the ground with a large hammer.

  • Designed player abilites including the optional characters
  • Programmed and developed first playable character ability
  • Implemented super meter functionality

Audio Design

Audio is a crucial part of video games, and I wanted it to be not only noticeable but also subtle. I created different audio cues for character movements based on the roughness and materials.

  • Finding royalty-free songs and audio
  • Applied various songs and sound effects to amplify nearby terrain and environment
  • Implemented a dynamic footstep system via blueprints

Minor Level Design

Even though I'm not a level designer, I took on the task of creating an eerie boss room for the second boss battle. I added bones and cages rattling around, and designed the boss to drop from the ceiling. Additionally, I created patrolling paths for added challenge.

Minor UI Design

Even though UI design wasn't my primary role in this project, I assisted my team by designing the boss UI and programming a stun meter into the game.

  • Designed the boss UI
  • Programmed and implemented the UI stun meter
  • Collaborated with the team to integrate UI elements seamlessly
